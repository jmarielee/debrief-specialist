# Jodi Paige-Lee, Intelligence Layer Designer, Interstitial Studios

### What's broken

The insight is sharpest the moment I ship, and it does not survive the trip to a blank page.

When a build lands, I know what I proved: the behavioral decision, the tradeoff, the constraint I solved, why it beats a generic AI demo. Days later the work is solid, but the angle has gone flat.

This is not a writing problem. It is a decay problem. Publishing while the angle is still sharp carries its own risk: shipping something that is not actually sharp, or crossing a line I cannot see at 10pm, like naming a client I am under NDA not to name. So I hesitate, the window closes, and the gap between what I build and what the market sees widens. That gap stands between me and the next role.

### What I've tried

Scattered notes, post-hoc rewriting. None of it holds. Notes capture the dump but leave it raw, useless three days later. The structuring has to happen inside the capture, not after.

### What I need

A folder-based specialist that takes a messy 200-500 word dump right after I ship and returns five reusable outputs, same session:

- LinkedIn post draft: a near-finished post I trim, claim-first
- Portfolio case study outline: problem, decision, constraint, system behavior, evidence, outcome
- Resume-ready sentence
- Pitch sentence: what this build proves about how I think
- Carousel SVGs: six 1080×1080px slide files, ready to take into Figma
