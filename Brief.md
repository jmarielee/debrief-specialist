# Jodi Paige-Lee, Intelligence Layer Designer, Interstitial Studios

### What's broken

The moment I ship a build, I know what I proved: the behavioral decision, the tradeoff, the constraint I solved, why it beats a generic AI demo. Days later the work is solid, but the sharp angle has dulled. The blank page gets bigger than the build.

This is not a writing problem. It is a momentum one. Right then, the angle is sharp. But publishing a strong point of view in public carries real risk: going viral for the wrong reason, crossing a line I cannot see at 10pm. So I hesitate, and as I do, the angle dulls and the blank page grows. The gap between what I build and what the market can see stands between me and the next role. Every stalled post widens it.

### What I've tried

Scattered notes, post-hoc rewriting. None of it holds. Notes capture the dump but leave it raw, and raw insight is useless. Structuring has to happen inside the capture, not after.

### What I need

A folder-based specialist that takes a messy 200-500 word dump right after I ship and returns five reusable outputs, same session:

- LinkedIn post draft: a near-finished post I trim, claim-first
- Portfolio case study outline: problem, decision, constraint, system behavior, evidence, outcome - ready to start
- Resume-ready sentence
- Pitch sentence: what this build proves about how I think
- Carousel SVGs: six 1080×1080px slide files, ready to take into Figma for editing
